AI Review

Experimental feature

CVWW 2026 will run a consent-based study that compares traditional human reviews with LLM-generated review. The experimental review is informational only and never influences acceptance decisions.

We will only generate an AI review for submissions whose authors explicitly consent in the submission system.

Why we're testing LLM reviews

Major AI venues such as AAAI and ICML are openly experimenting with large language models (LLMs) to assist peer review. CVWW would like to understand, with similar care, whether authors find LLM feedback helpful, trustworthy, or complete, and which concerns persist around accuracy and confidentiality.

Evidence is still scarce, so we are launching an optional pilot that compares how authors perceive traditional human reviews versus an additional, LLM-generated review. Your consent lets us evaluate whether such tools can responsibly complement — not replace — expert judgment within our community.

What changes for consenting authors

  • After you explicitly opt in, the PC/AC team will generate one extra review of your paper using a selected LLM.
  • The LLM-generated review never factors into acceptance or rejection decisions and does not replace human reviewers.
  • Consenting authors receive a short post-review questionnaire to compare the usefulness, accuracy, trustworthiness, and completeness of human and LLM feedback.

The experimental review is produced solely for this research study, and it will be clearly labeled so you can distinguish it from human feedback.

Author consent and transparency

  • You will see a clear consent item during submission. If you do not consent, your process remains unchanged.
  • Consent can be withheld without penalty, and opting out simply prevents us from running the AI review for your paper.
  • After reviews are released, consenting authors will receive the brief anonymous questionnaire to reflect on both review types.

If you decline consent, nothing is shared with an AI service and you only receive the standard CVWW 2026 reviews.

Confidentiality & data handling

  • Generating an LLM review may involve transmitting manuscript text to a third-party AI service or to a secure, self-hosted model. We are prioritizing privacy modes and leaning toward locally hosted options whenever feasible.
  • Only the minimum necessary text will be shared, and we will clearly document which service was used for transparency.

We are still finalizing the exact deployment setup, but privacy-preserving settings and access controls will be in place before any manuscript leaves CMT.

How we will share findings

We plan to feed the lessons from this study back to the CVWW community through an in-venue presentation, a concise public write-up, and potentially a dedicated publication. Insights will help shape future review policies and best practices.

Our goal is to provide the broader community with evidence about when AI-generated feedback feels useful, where it falls short, and how to address remaining trust, accuracy, and confidentiality gaps.
